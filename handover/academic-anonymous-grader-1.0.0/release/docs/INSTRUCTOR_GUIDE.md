# Instructor Guide

## Overview

As an Instructor, you grade anonymous submissions without ever seeing student identities. This guide explains how to access your assigned assessments, claim submissions, and enter grades.

## Accessing Assigned Assessments

- Log in with your Instructor account.
- Navigate to the **Grading** page.
- You will see only materials and assessments that an Administrator has assigned to you.
- Assessments you are not assigned to will not appear in dropdowns.

## Grading Claims (Locking)

To prevent multiple instructors from grading the same submission simultaneously:

1. **Claim a submission** by selecting it in the grading list. This locks it for your exclusive editing.
2. The claim expires after **120 minutes** of inactivity.
3. **Renew your claim** by continuing to edit — each save extends the expiration.
4. **Release your claim** when you finish grading or want to step away.

### Who Can Edit

| Scenario | Result |
|---|---|
| You claim a submission | ✅ You can edit |
| Another instructor tries to edit your active claim | ❌ "Currently being graded by another instructor" |
| Your claim has expired | ✅ Anyone can reclaim |
| Administrator releases your claim | ✅ Anyone can claim |

## Grading Workflow

1. Select your material and assessment.
2. Select a submission from the list.
3. Enter grades and feedback for each question.
4. **Save Draft** to preserve work without finalizing.
5. **Mark Submission Graded** when all questions have grades (validates completeness).
6. If a reviewer returns the submission for correction, you'll see a **Needs Correction** badge.

## Progress Monitoring

Your progress is shown on the Grading page:
- **Total**: All submissions assigned to this assessment
- **Ungraded**: Not yet started
- **In Progress**: Drafts in progress
- **Graded**: Completed
- **Completion %**: Graded / Total × 100

## Privacy Rules

- You never see student names, emails, or institutional IDs.
- You only see anonymous codes (e.g., `STU-ABCD1234`).
- All grading actions are logged for audit without exposing identities.
- Identity data is encrypted at rest and only decrypted during approved exports.
